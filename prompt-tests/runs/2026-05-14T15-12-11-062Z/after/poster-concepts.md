# Poster Concepts: Key Art Pass

## One-Sheet Thesis

The one-sheet problem is scale control. The art has to sell a multiverse without letting the film float away from Evelyn’s real pressures: the receipt-covered apartment table in scene 2, Joy staring into the laundromat machine in scene 3, the divorce papers carried through scenes 2, 14, 17, and 118, and the family linking arms to pull Joy back from the Bagel in scene 118. The strongest key art should make infinity feel like it is pressing through a laundromat, an IRS cubicle, or a family document, not arriving as generic space spectacle.

## Primary Directions

### 1. Receipt Portal

**One-sheet logic:**  
Make the audit table the whole universe. Evelyn is trapped inside the paperwork before she is trapped inside the multiverse.

**Composition:**  
Top-down view of the apartment dining table from scene 2. Tax documents, receipts, calculator, rice cooker edge, and hidden divorce papers spiral into a circular hole at center. Evelyn sits small at the table’s edge, boxed in by paper. The spiral can echo the round mirror in scene 1, the tumbling laundry machine in scene 3, and the Bagel revealed in scenes 67 and 91.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 2 gives the table covered in years of tax documents and receipts, plus hidden divorce papers. Scene 15 brings receipts back inside the IRS audit. Scene 67 has Jobu opening her hands to reveal the swirling Bagel. Scene 118 turns the Bagel into the thing Joy must be pulled back from.

**Typography:**  
Title set like a stamped IRS form, slightly misregistered, sitting on top of the paper field. Credits can feel like filing labels, but keep them clean enough to avoid parody.

**Thumbnail read:**  
A woman at a tiny paper island being pulled toward a black circular center.

**What not to include:**  
No galaxy background. No floating heads. No generic portal lightning. The menace must come from receipts, divorce papers, and the audit world.

### 2. Laundromat Eye

**One-sheet logic:**  
Use the washing machine as the film’s first honest portal: Joy’s absence, Evelyn’s routine, and the Bagel shape all in one object.

**Composition:**  
A laundromat washer door fills the poster as a round frame. Inside the glass: tumbling laundry from scene 3 forms a rough black-and-white vortex. Joy’s blank reflection is visible in the glass. Evelyn stands behind her, out of focus, holding the baseball bat from scene 6. A small googly eye stuck to laundry plastic sits near the lower corner.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 3 has Joy staring blankly into a tumbling laundry machine until Becky kisses her. Scene 6 gives the laundromat, the baseball bat, the $10 roll of quarters, and googly eyes on laundry plastic. Scene 110 pays off the googly eye as Evelyn’s third eye and as bullets turned harmless.

**Typography:**  
Title curved subtly around the washer rim, like machine labeling. Keep it worn, practical, and slightly off-center.

**Thumbnail read:**  
A circular washer door becomes an eye, with Joy’s face caught in it.

**What not to include:**  
Do not make the laundromat cute or retro. No rainbow clothes explosion. The washer should feel like a place Joy disappears into.

**AI image prompt:**  
Theatrical one-sheet, close-up laundromat washing machine door as a circular portal, tumbling laundry forming a dark vortex behind glass, reflection of a young woman in worn hoodie staring blankly into it, older Chinese woman in oversized t-shirt and thin glasses blurred behind holding a baseball bat, small googly eye on laundry plastic near the frame, grounded laundromat lighting, old machines, laundry carts, tense family mood, no sci-fi stars.

### 3. Fanny Pack Audit

**One-sheet logic:**  
Turn the IRS into an action poster, but the weapon is still a husband’s ridiculous everyday object.

**Composition:**  
Alpha Waymond in the IRS 10th floor cubicle, mid-swing with the oversized fanny pack from scene 17. Cubicle walls break behind him. Security batons, fish bowl pebbles, receipts, and divorce papers streak through the motion path. Evelyn is seated in the audit chair, shoes wrong-footed under the desk from scene 15, staring in shock.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 14 sets up the fanny pack, bluetooth earpieces, compact umbrella over the elevator camera, and divorce papers. Scene 15 has Evelyn switching shoes under Deirdre’s desk. Scene 17 has Alpha Waymond eating chapstick and using the fanny pack as nunchucks against security guards.

**Typography:**  
Title in hard office fluorescent white, cut by the fanny pack motion arc. Credits can sit like the bottom of an IRS notice.

**Thumbnail read:**  
A man weaponizes a fanny pack in a beige cubicle battlefield.

**What not to include:**  
No superhero pose. No tactical armor. Waymond’s polo, transition lenses, and ordinary body language are the joke and the point.

**AI image prompt:**

### 4. The Bagel Is a Family Document

**One-sheet logic:**  
Make the Bagel less like a cosmic object and more like a private wound made visible.

**Composition:**  
A black Bagel-shaped void burns through divorce papers. Around the torn circle are handwritten verse-jump instructions from scene 14 and the marriage wound from scenes 2, 17, and 118. Evelyn and Waymond’s hands hold opposite sides of the paper. Joy’s hand reaches from the darkness but is not fully visible.

**Script evidence:**  
Divorce papers appear in scenes 2, 14, 17, and 118. Scene 14 needs writing on the back for verse jumping instructions. Scene 67 puts the swirling Bagel in Jobu’s hands. Scene 118 has Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong linking arms to pull Joy out of the darkness.

**Typography:**  
Small, severe title below the paper, almost like a legal caption. Let the paper carry the drama.

**Thumbnail read:**  
A divorce form has a black hole burned through it, with hands trying to hold it together.

**What not to include:**  
No literal courtroom, no wedding imagery, no explanatory multiverse diagrams.

**AI image prompt:**

### 5. Two Rocks, One Family

**One-sheet logic:**  
Use the quietest image as the boldest campaign break. The poster trusts the film’s emotional silence.

**Composition:**  
Empty dirt field from scene 94. Two rocks sit near a cliff edge with vast negative space. Tiny text-message-style lines can sit above them, but keep it spare. In the lower margin, almost hidden, place a small karaoke microphone from scene 118 or a tiny receipt scrap to tether the rock universe back to the family.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 94 is an empty dirt field with two silent rocks conversing through on-screen text. Scene 118 includes the karaoke microphone while the family pulls Joy from the Bagel. Scene 1 begins with the family awkwardly singing karaoke in a hazy mirror.

**Typography:**  
Very small title, low on the poster, with generous empty space. No big title treatment fighting the rocks.

**Thumbnail read:**  
Two rocks alone on a pale field.

**What not to include:**  
No faces in the sky. No cosmic portal. No desert adventure language. The emptiness is the concept.

**AI image prompt:**  
Restrained art-house theatrical one-sheet, empty windswept dirt field under plain daylight, two prominent rocks near a cliff edge, vast negative space, tiny karaoke microphone half-buried near lower edge as subtle family object, quiet composition, no characters, no spectacle, no galaxies, no dramatic clouds, clean poster layout.

### 6. Googly Eye Mercy

**One-sheet logic:**  
Turn the film’s absurd prop into a moral image: Evelyn chooses to disarm the battle instead of winning it.

**Composition:**  
Evelyn front-facing in the IRS 9th floor atrium from scene 110. A googly eye sits on her forehead. Incoming bullets have turned into googly eyes midair. Behind her: crushed banister, office supply robot exoskeleton shape, belt whip motion, and the baseball bat with a googly eye. Her expression is calm, not triumphant.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 6 introduces googly eyes on laundry plastic and the baseball bat. Scene 110 has Evelyn sticking a googly eye on her forehead and turning bullets into harmless googly eyes. Scene 101 has Waymond bleeding and asking everyone to be kind in the same IRS battlefield context.

**Typography:**  
Rounded but not cute. Title can sit around the forehead eye as a visual axis, with credits locked to the bottom like office signage.

**Thumbnail read:**  
Evelyn with a third googly eye, bullets becoming harmless dots.

**What not to include:**  
Do not turn this into a gag poster. No smiley chaos wallpaper. The absurdity should feel like a choice made during violence.

**AI image prompt:**  
Theatrical one-sheet, middle-aged Chinese woman in oversized t-shirt and thin glasses standing calm in IRS 9th floor atrium battlefield, small googly eye placed on her forehead like a third eye, bullets transforming into harmless googly eyes around her, crushed banister and office cubicle debris behind, baseball bat with googly eye visible, fluorescent office light, emotional restraint, absurd object treated seriously, no cartoon style.

### 7. White Veil, Black Center

**One-sheet logic:**  
Let Jobu’s threat feel ceremonial and intimate rather than just chaotic.

**Composition:**  
The empress hall from scene 91: massive white veils fill the frame, ripped open by Jobu dressed as alien royalty. The Bagel is visible as a black circular center behind the fabric. Evelyn is small in the foreground, almost swallowed by white cloth and wind.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 91 places Jobu in the Temple Universe, dressed as alien royalty, ripping open a massive white veil to reveal the deafening Bagel. Scene 67 first shows the Bagel in Jobu’s hands. Scene 118 makes that same darkness the force pulling Joy away.

**Typography:**  
Thin, high-contrast type integrated into the veil folds. Avoid horror typography. The image already has threat.

**Thumbnail read:**  
White fabric opens onto a black circle.

**What not to include:**  
No occult symbols beyond the Bagel. No generic cult robes. The white veils and Jobu’s alien royalty are enough.

**AI image prompt:**

### 8. The Pull

**One-sheet logic:**  
Build the final one-sheet around contact: not multiverse options, but a family physically refusing to let Joy disappear.

**Composition:**  
A vertical chain of bodies and arms from scene 118. Joy is being pulled from a black Bagel darkness at the top edge. Evelyn anchors the center. Waymond and Gong Gong link arms below her. Echoes of other worlds appear only as small fragments caught around the bodies: karaoke mirror glow from scene 1, receipts from scene 2, laundromat washer circle from scene 3, IRS red sticky notes from scene 124.

**Script evidence:**  
Scene 118 has Jobu sucked into the Bagel while Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong link arms to pull her out. Scene 1 gives the family karaoke mirror image. Scene 2 gives receipts and divorce papers. Scene 124 ends with Evelyn holding Waymond’s hand in the IRS office, with red sticky notes nearby.

**Typography:**  
Title locked beneath the human chain, steady and unshowy. Let the arms create the poster’s movement.

**Thumbnail read:**  
A family chain pulling one person out of a black circle.

**What not to include:**  
No ensemble collage of every universe. No victory pose. The bodies should look strained, awkward, and specific.

**AI image prompt:**  
Emotional theatrical one-sheet, family physically linking arms to pull a young woman out of dark bagel-shaped void, middle-aged Chinese mother anchoring the center, husband and elderly father below holding on, fragments of receipts, red IRS sticky notes, karaoke mirror glow, and laundromat washer circle around them, grounded human strain, dark background, practical key art composition, no generic superhero ensemble, no cosmic stars.

## Teaser Directions

### 1. Googly Eye on Black

A single googly eye centered on a black field, but the white plastic rim is scuffed like it came from the laundromat in scene 6. A tiny receipt corner sits under it. This tees up scene 110 without explaining it.

### 2. Wrong Shoes Under the Desk

A cropped IRS audit desk from scene 15: Evelyn’s shoes switched to the wrong feet under fluorescent light, receipts hanging over the desk edge, Deirdre’s hand grip strengthener visible above. No faces. The multiverse begins as absurd compliance.

### 3. Two Rocks

Use scene 94 almost literally: two rocks in an empty dirt field near the cliff edge. The only campaign intrusion is the title, small enough that the rocks remain the joke and the ache.

## Series Logic

The campaign should move from domestic pressure to cosmic choice. Start with teasers that make ordinary objects feel charged: the wrong shoes from scene 15, the googly eye from scenes 6 and 110, the two rocks from scene 94. The first full one-sheets should stay inside recognizable spaces, especially the receipt-covered apartment table in scene 2, the laundromat washer in scene 3, and the IRS cubicles in scenes 15 through 17. Later art can reveal the larger mythic images: Jobu tearing open the white veil in scene 91, the Bagel shown in scenes 67 and 91, and the family chain in scene 118. The sequence should teach the audience that the same circular shape keeps changing meaning: mirror, washer, Bagel, googly eye, family attention.

## Best Bets

1. **Receipt Portal**  
Best overall because it solves the film in one image: Evelyn’s actual life in scene 2 becomes the portal. It uses receipts, hidden divorce papers, the apartment table, and the Bagel logic without leaving the domestic world.

2. **The Pull**  
Best emotional one-sheet. Scene 118 gives the campaign a clean physical action: Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong linking arms to pull Joy from darkness. It tells the audience what the movie finally chooses.

3. **Laundromat Eye**  
Best teaser-to-main bridge. Scene 3 gives Joy staring into the washer, scene 6 gives the baseball bat and googly eyes, and the circular washer shape can quietly prepare the Bagel without making the art feel like generic multiverse imagery.
