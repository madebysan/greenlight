# Creative Director Read

## Core Thesis

This is a film about a woman whose life feels too crowded to enter fully, until the multiverse literalizes that crowding. Evelyn begins boxed in by tax receipts, hidden divorce papers, overcooked noodles, and a daughter she cannot acknowledge cleanly in scenes 2, 4, and 6. The viewing experience should keep collapsing the cosmic into the domestic: a fanny pack fight in scene 17, a Bagel in Jobu's hands in scene 67, two silent rocks in scene 94, then a family physically linking arms to pull Joy back in scene 118. The point is not that everything matters equally; it is that Evelyn chooses this one messy room, this husband, this daughter, this audit, after seeing everything else.

## Recurring Anchors

- **Circular portals of attention:** The round mirror karaoke image in scene 1, tumbling laundry in scene 3, the Bagel in scenes 67 and 91, and googly eyes in scenes 6 and 110 should feel like competing ways to look at the same life.

- **Paperwork as emotional pressure:** Tax documents and receipts trap Evelyn at the apartment table in scene 2, receipts return inside the IRS audit in scene 15, and divorce papers surface across scenes 2, 14, 17, and 118 as the marriage's private wound.

- **Everyday objects becoming weapons or rituals:** The compact umbrella hides the elevator camera in scene 14, the fanny pack becomes Alpha Waymond's combat tool in scene 17, the Auditor trophy becomes a jump pad in scene 80, and the baseball bat gains a googly eye in scene 110.

- **Joy as absence and overload:** Joy stares into a washing machine in scene 3, is misintroduced as Becky's "good friend" in scene 6, returns as Jobu in extravagant threat mode in scenes 56 and 91, then becomes the body everyone pulls out of darkness in scene 118.

- **The IRS as emotional battlefield:** The bland cubicles, files, trophy, security, and breakaway walls of scenes 15 through 18 turn bureaucracy into combat, while the 9th floor atrium in scenes 86, 101, and 110 becomes the place where Waymond's bleeding plea for kindness changes the rules of the fight.

## Department North Star

Protect the collision between clutter and infinity. Production design should keep the apartment, laundromat, RV, and IRS full of specific pressures, receipts, rice cooker, laundry carts, cables, cubicles, trophies, sticky notes, so the cosmic never floats away from Evelyn's real life. Costume, props, stunts, VFX, sound, and edit should treat absurdity as emotional evidence: Jobu's pig and Elvis jumpsuit in scene 56, hot dog fingers in scene 62, the white-veiled Bagel hall in scene 91, the silent rock field in scene 94, and the googly-eye bullets in scene 110 all need to land as expressions of despair, tenderness, or choice, not random spectacle.
