# Locations: Scout Pass

## Scout Thesis

The locations should make Evelyn's life feel physically overfull before the multiverse arrives, then keep proving that the cosmic pressure is still coming from family rooms, work rooms, laundry rooms, and bureaucratic rooms. The apartment dining room traps her under tax documents, receipts, overcooked noodles, a rice cooker, security feed monitors, and a childhood photo of Joy in scenes 2, 4, and 12. The laundromat turns Joy's private absence into an everyday image when she stares into a tumbling machine in scene 3, then turns family shame public when Evelyn misintroduces Becky in scene 6. The IRS should not feel like a neutral office; by scenes 15 through 18, its cubicles, files, trophy, closet, cameras, and breakaway walls have become the place where divorce papers, receipts, security guards, Jobu, and Alpha Waymond all collide.

## Location System

The location plan should group the world by pressure, not geography.

Domestic pressure starts in the apartment dining/living room. In scene 1, the hazy mirror karaoke image gives us a fragile memory of the family as one unit. By scene 2, the same general world is clogged with years of tax documents and receipts, and by scene 4, Evelyn is dumping soy sauce and scallions into overcooked noodles while avoiding Becky. The scout priority is not "small apartment"; it is a space where love has nowhere to land cleanly.

Labor and family visibility live in the laundromat. Scene 3 gives Joy's blank stare into the tumbling machine and Becky's kiss. Scene 6 gives the dented air vent, baseball bat handle, Gong Gong, Becky being called a "good friend," and the wound that follows. The location needs working machines, carts, signage, and sightlines that let private family damage happen in public.

Transition and rupture belong to vehicles, elevators, stairwells, and closets. The laundry RV in scene 13 carries racks of clothing and cookies before scene 23 turns the same footprint into a wired Alphaverse control room with a shrine to Alpha Evelyn. The IRS elevator in scene 14 needs the compact umbrella covering the security camera to feel like a tiny practical action that opens the story's rules.

The IRS is the battlefield of adulthood. Scene 15 has Evelyn switching shoes under Deirdre's desk while receipts and audit dread sit above her. Scene 17 turns the same office into fanny-pack combat after the divorce papers surface. Scene 56 makes Jobu's arrival grotesquely specific: Elvis jumpsuit, pig on a leash, confetti guard, changing picture frames. The 9th floor atrium later has to hold Waymond bleeding and asking everyone to be kind in scene 101, then googly-eye bullets and a mech suit in scene 110.

Cosmic spaces should be few, legible, and emotionally exact. The Empress Hall in scene 91 is the Bagel's ceremonial void: alien royalty, white veils, wind, and a deafening swirl. The rock field in scene 94 is the opposite: two rocks, dirt field, cliff edge, silence, and subtitle dialogue. These places should feel like emotional arguments, not scenic variety.

## Priority Scout Briefs

### Apartment Dining Room / Living Room

**Story job:** Establish the life Evelyn cannot fully enter because it is already too crowded. Scene 1 gives a hazy mirror image of the family singing karaoke together, so the room must be capable of warmth. Scene 2 flips that warmth into overwhelm with years of tax documents and receipts across the table. Scene 4 adds the overcooked noodles, soy sauce, scallions, and Evelyn's avoidance of Becky. Scene 12 isolates Evelyn with a childhood photo of Joy inside the same chaos.

**Visual personality:** Tight, layered, and practical. The listed workout equipment, self-help books, old TV, security feed, rice cooker, microwave, tax papers, and receipts should form a real domestic ecosystem, not decorative mess.

**Practical needs:** Controllable dressing density, working microwave and rice cooker, security feed monitors, table coverage for paperwork, room for camera to find the mirror image in scene 1 and the childhood photo beat in scene 12.

**Scenes:** 1, 2, 4, 12.

**Risk:** If the room is merely cluttered, it becomes visual noise. The scout should prioritize a layout where receipts, food, screens, and family objects can each be read clearly during emotional beats.

### Laundromat

**Story job:** Make the family's private failures happen in a public work space. Scene 3 needs Joy staring blankly into a tumbling laundry machine until Becky kisses her. Scene 6 needs Evelyn thumping a dented air vent with a baseball bat handle and then hurting Joy by introducing Becky as a "good friend." Scene 62 returns the laundromat as the Hot Dog universe, so the place must also absorb absurd physical comedy.

**Visual personality:** Eclectic but not cute. Laundry carts, old arcade cabinet, TV playing a musical, wash-and-fold signs, machines, vents, and breakaway front window glass should all feel like daily labor before they become icons.

**Practical needs:** Functional tumbling machines, strong foreground/background sightlines for Joy and Becky, practical air vent area, space for family clustering around Gong Gong, breakaway front window glass, controllable reflections and machine motion.

**Scenes:** 3, 6, 62.

**Risk:** A too-polished laundromat weakens the Joy beat. A too-cramped one may block the scene 6 family traffic and scene 62 prosthetic/VFX work.

### Laundry RV / Alphaverse RV

**Story job:** Bridge the family's mundane business with the film's sci-fi machinery. Scene 13 is practical and tense: Evelyn drives a converted RV filled with racks of clothing while Waymond holds Tupperware cookies. Scene 23 turns the same footprint into an Alphaverse control room with wires, screens, Alpha Waymond in a rigged chair, and a shrine to deceased Alpha Evelyn.

**Visual personality:** It should feel repurposed, not designed from scratch. Clothing racks and laundry bags are the base reality; tech cabling, monitors, and the Evelyn shrine should look grafted onto that reality.

**Practical needs:** Driving rig, removable or modular dressing for the Alphaverse build, monitor arrays, heavy cabling, chair rig, shrine area, room for smoke/equipment shutdown continuity if tied into scene 86's Alphaverse action.

**Scenes:** 13, 23, connected visually to 86.

**Risk:** This location can become generic sci-fi. Preserve the laundry infrastructure so Alpha tech still feels born from Evelyn's life, not from a separate movie.

### IRS Elevator / Lobby Threshold

**Story job:** Deliver the first clean rupture. Scene 14 begins as corporate movement through the IRS, then Waymond opens a compact umbrella to cover the security camera and becomes Alpha Waymond. Bluetooth devices burn red and Evelyn's life paths flash by.

**Visual personality:** Bland, controlled, surveilled. The elevator should feel so ordinary that the umbrella action becomes alarming.

**Practical needs:** Working doors, camera placement, practical lighting changes with bright light through cracks, clean geometry for the umbrella blocking the camera, controllable reflective surfaces, VFX-friendly lighting for device glow and montage interruption.

**Scenes:** 14.

**Risk:** If the elevator is too stylized, Alpha Waymond's break in behavior loses contrast. The location should stay deadpan.

### IRS 10th Floor Cubicles

**Story job:** Turn paperwork into combat. Scene 15 has the audit pressure, Deirdre talking, receipts, Evelyn switching shoes to the wrong feet under the desk, and the first jump pad. Scene 17 exposes divorce papers immediately before Alpha Waymond eats chapstick and uses his fanny pack as nunchucks against security guards. Scene 18 lets Jobu enter the office game-like, playing "Duck, Duck, Goose" with hostages before killing Closet Evelyn. Scene 56 escalates with Jobu in an Elvis jumpsuit, pig on a leash, a guard turned to confetti, salsa-dancer human shield, luchador pile drive, and changing picture frames.

**Visual personality:** Bureaucratic beige under stress: files, desks, cubicle walls, audit trophy, framed photos, paperwork, and fluorescent sameness that can be broken open.

**Practical needs:** Breakaway cubicle walls, stunt-safe desks, file dressing, security guard pathways, trophy placement, elevator access or matched entrance for Jobu, picture frames suitable for VFX replacement, space for fanny-pack choreography.

**Scenes:** 15, 16, 17, 18, 56.

**Risk:** This is likely the main schedule sink. The set must survive reset-heavy combat, prop continuity, VFX inserts, and multiple emotional tones without becoming unreadable.

### Janitor's Closet

**Story job:** Give the multiverse its first cramped, dirty instruction room. Scene 16 splits Evelyn between the audit and a dark closet conversation with Alpha Waymond, ending with hands bursting through the closet door and snapping his neck.

**Visual personality:** Dim, utility-cluttered, physically close. It should feel like the least glamorous place possible to explain infinite realities.

**Practical needs:** Breakaway door, tight but shootable walls, utility dressing, controlled practical light, matching geography to the cubicle split-screen.

**Scenes:** 16.

**Risk:** Too small and the scene becomes unshootable; too large and the comedy of cosmic exposition inside a utility closet disappears.

### IRS 8th Floor / Stairwell Run

**Story job:** Keep verse-jumping frantic and physically ridiculous. Scene 25 uses open cubicles, bulletin board, stairwell access, a breakaway exit sign, filing cabinets as barricades, Alpha Waymond trying to give himself four papercuts, and Deirdre flipping a table before pro-wrestling attacks. Scene 48 continues the building's vertical pressure as Deirdre leaps to crush Evelyn and Evelyn jumps through "I LOVE YOU" into a kung fu deflection.

**Visual personality:** A slightly different bureaucratic layer from the 10th floor: open enough to run, cluttered enough to weaponize.

**Practical needs:** Stunt-rated flooring, table flip zone, filing cabinet barricades, breakaway exit sign, stairwell access, drywall break or matched stunt wall for Deirdre's impact in scene 48.

**Scenes:** 25, 48.

**Risk:** Repetition with the 10th floor. The scout should find a layout that clearly reads as another level of the same institution while giving choreography new geometry.

### IRS 9th Floor Executive Office / Panic Room

**Story job:** Show Evelyn's mastery through hidden institutional weirdness. Scene 67 uses the 9th floor hallway for Jobu revealing the swirling Bagel in her hands before Alpha Gong Gong crashes through a wall in a tricked-out wheelchair. Scene 80 moves through the executive office and panic room, with a secret bookshelf door, tear gas, adult prop dressing, vents, cocaine, porno mags, Xbox, and Evelyn bursting out to fight with a riot shield like a sign spinner. Scene 85 adds strong pinky fingers breaking wheelchair pegs and flicking attackers across the room. Scene 124 resolves with Evelyn holding Waymond's hand, briefly distracted by the multiverse, then smiling back at Deirdre.

**Visual personality:** More expensive than the cubicles, but morally stranger. The office should look like institutional authority; the panic room should expose private absurdity behind it.

**Practical needs:** Secret bookshelf door, panic room with vents for gas, tear gas practical effects, adult prop dressing control, stunt-safe wall zones, wheelchair stunt access, riot shield choreography space, clean reset for the quiet resolution in scene 124.

**Scenes:** 67, 80, 85, 124.

**Risk:** Tone control. The panic room's adult props are specific, but they should support Evelyn's chaotic ascension in scene 80 rather than become a detachable gag wall.

### IRS 9th Floor Atrium

**Story job:** Become the emotional battlefield where violence changes rules. Scene 86 has Alpha Waymond killed in the Alphaverse as his IRS body goes limp before kissing Evelyn, and Evelyn vomits orange soda and a frog paperweight. Scene 101 centers bleeding Waymond pleading for kindness amid the battlefield. Scene 110 turns the fight toward healing: Evelyn puts a googly eye on her forehead, bullets become googly eyes, she rides on Chad's shoulders, and Alpha Gong Gong's mech suit enters.

**Visual personality:** Large, public, and exposed. The atrium should feel like the building's heart, with enough height, banisters, and break room adjacency for the fight to scale up.

**Practical needs:** Wide choreography area, crushable banisters for mech suit action, VFX sightlines for bullet-to-googly-eye work, rigging potential, safe floor for crowd movement, break room access, reset zones for blood, soda vomit, frog paperweight, and battle debris.

**Scenes:** 86, 101, 110.

**Risk:** This is the other major schedule sink. It has stunts, VFX, emotional speeches, crowd behavior, mech impact, and continuity across multiple universe intercuts.

### Empress Hall

**Story job:** Give Jobu's nihilism ceremonial scale. Scene 91 has Jobu dressed as alien royalty ripping open massive white veils to reveal the deafening, swirling Bagel. This is not just a temple; it is the physical form of temptation.

**Visual personality:** White, veiled, wind-driven, and severe. The hall should feel stripped down compared with the IRS clutter, but still tactile through fabric and air movement.

**Practical needs:** Large white veils, wind machines, Bagel VFX/practical reference space, rigging for fabric movement, strong acoustic or visual plan for the "deafening" force, clean floor and depth for Jobu's reveal.

**Scenes:** 91, tied to 67 and 118 through Bagel imagery.

**Risk:** Empty whiteness can become generic. The veils and wind must be scouted as active physical elements, not background dressing.

### Rock Field

**Story job:** Strip the film down to almost nothing. Scene 94 needs two silent rocks in an empty windswept dirt field, with subtitle dialogue, as a quiet contrast to the IRS fights and Bagel spectacle. Scene 118 adds the rock chase on a cliff as part of the family pull across universes.

**Visual personality:** Bare dirt, horizon, wind, two readable rocks, and a cliff edge. The emptiness is the point.

**Practical needs:** Two prominent rocks, safe cliff-edge set piece or controlled match, wind continuity, subtitle-friendly clean compositions, minimal background distractions, access for a small unit.

**Scenes:** 94, 118.

**Risk:** The field cannot be merely pretty. It needs enough emptiness to make two rocks feel like a devastating reduction of Evelyn and Joy, while still being safe for the cliff/chase component in scene 118.

## Company Moves & Friction

The IRS block will punish the schedule most because the same institutional world has to host audit stillness, combat, VFX, emotional climax, and resolution. Scenes 15 through 18 start with Deirdre's desk, receipts, wrong-foot shoes, closet split-screen, fanny-pack fighting, divorce papers, breakaway cubicles, and Jobu's hostage game. Scene 56 returns with Elvis Jobu, pig, confetti, picture-frame changes, and more stunt escalation. Scenes 80, 85, 86, 101, 110, and 124 then move through executive office, panic room, atrium, gas, riot shield work, wheelchair impacts, orange soda vomit, kindness speech, googly-eye bullets, mech suit, and final calm.

The apartment and laundromat are emotionally non-negotiable but mechanically dense. The apartment must reset between the hazy karaoke image in scene 1, receipt overload in scene 2, noodle action in scene 4, and Joy photo exhaustion in scene 12. The laundromat must run tumbling machines for Joy's scene 3 stare, hold the family shame scene with Gong Gong and Becky in scene 6, and later support Hot Dog universe prosthetic/VFX work in scene 62.

The RV creates a build problem because scene 13 and scene 23 need the same basic footprint to read as both laundry vehicle and Alphaverse control room. The scout should assume a modular dressing strategy: racks, clothing, cookies, and laundry bags first; monitor arrays, cables, chair rig, and Evelyn shrine second.

The Empress Hall and rock field are isolated but high-concept. Scene 91 depends on veils, wind, alien royalty framing, and a Bagel reveal. Scene 94 depends on silence, two rocks, and negative space. They may be short page counts, but the location choices carry major thematic weight.

Scene 118 is the most cross-location editorial burden: the family links arms to pull Joy from the Bagel while the script calls for wind pulling characters, a rock chase near a cliff, and rapid cutting across 10+ universes resolving simultaneously. The location plan should keep all major worlds visually distinct enough that the intercutting remains legible.

## Exterior / Night / Crowd Risk

- **Night interior control:** The apartment opens at night in scene 1 with a hazy mirror karaoke image. Reflections, haze, practical TV/security monitors, and small-room lighting need control so the family image reads clearly.

- **Exterior day rock field:** Scene 94 needs an empty dirt field with two rocks and wind, while scene 118 needs a cliff/chase component. Weather, horizon clutter, safety, and subtitle readability are all risks.

- **Large interior crowd/stunt risk:** The IRS 9th floor atrium in scenes 86, 101, and 110 needs battle choreography, banisters crushed by a mech suit, Waymond bleeding, bullets turning into googly eyes, and multiple bodies in motion.

- **Office stunt reset risk:** The 10th floor cubicles in scenes 17, 18, and 56 require fanny-pack martial arts, security guards, hostages, confetti VFX, picture-frame changes, and breakaway walls.

- **Practical effects risk:** Tear gas in scene 80, wind machines and massive veils in scene 91, orange soda and frog paperweight vomit in scene 86, and breakaway laundromat glass in the location requirements all need location permissions and reset plans.

- **Animal/prop complication:** Scene 56 includes Jobu with a pig on a leash. The 10th floor location needs safe animal pathing, quiet holding, and flooring that can be reset.

- **Vehicle access:** The laundry RV in scenes 13 and 23 needs a driving rig and enough access for clothing racks, cabling, monitors, and chair rig installation.

- **Crowd containment:** IRS scenes involving security guards, hostages, Alpha forces, and battle movement need holding space outside the shooting floor. This matters especially for scenes 17, 18, 56, 80, 85, 101, and 110.

## What To Preserve

1. Preserve the apartment as a pressure cooker, not a sitcom living room. Scene 2's tax documents and receipts, scene 4's overcooked noodles with soy sauce and scallions, and scene 12's childhood photo of Joy must all belong to the same suffocating space.

2. Preserve the laundromat's working-machine reality. Joy staring into the tumbling machine in scene 3 and Evelyn using a baseball bat handle on the dented vent in scene 6 need tactile labor around them.

3. Preserve the IRS as bland bureaucracy before it becomes spectacle. The wrong-foot shoes under Deirdre's desk in scene 15, divorce papers before the fanny-pack fight in scene 17, and Waymond's bleeding plea for kindness in scene 101 only work if the office remains recognizably ordinary.

4. Preserve the contrast between the Empress Hall and the rock field. Scene 91 needs white veils, wind, alien royalty, and the Bagel's scale. Scene 94 needs two rocks, dirt, quiet, and almost nothing else.

5. Preserve object-specific absurdity. The compact umbrella in scene 14, chapstick and fanny pack in scene 17, pig and Elvis jumpsuit in scene 56, Auditor trophy/sign-spinner logic around scene 80, Bagel in scenes 67 and 91, and googly eye in scene 110 are location-relevant anchors, not throwaway props.
