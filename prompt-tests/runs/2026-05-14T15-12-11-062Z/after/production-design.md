# Production Design: Designer Pass

## Material World

The physical language should make every universe feel like it has been squeezed out of Evelyn’s existing life rather than imported from outside it. The apartment is already a pressure cooker in scene 2: tax documents, receipts, workout equipment, rice cooker, calculator, and hidden divorce papers crowd the table while Evelyn sits overwhelmed. The laundromat extends that pressure through circular machines and practical labor: Joy stares into a tumbling washer in scene 3, Evelyn uses a baseball bat on a dented air vent in scene 6, and googly eyes sit on laundry plastic before returning as Evelyn’s third eye and bullet transformation in scene 110. The IRS should not become a generic office battlefield; it should remain a bureaucracy made violent, with receipts and cookies in scene 15, trophies and closet doors in scene 16, fanny pack combat in scene 17, a panic room full of inappropriate adult dressing in scene 80, and the 9th floor atrium becoming the emotional battlefield where bleeding Waymond asks everyone to be kind in scene 101.

## Set Dressing Rules

1. **Apartment clutter must read as accumulated obligation, not random mess.** In scene 2, prioritize tax documents, receipts, calculator, rice cooker, workout equipment, and hidden divorce papers so Evelyn’s stress is visible before anyone explains it. In scene 12, keep the family portraits and single receipt active around her blank stare at Joy’s childhood photo, so the room feels like memory and paperwork fighting for the same space.

2. **The laundromat should make circular attention unavoidable.** Scene 3 needs functional tumbling machines strong enough to hold Joy’s blank focus until Becky interrupts it. Scene 6 should carry smaller circles and tokens: the $10 roll of quarters, googly eyes on laundry plastic, the baby carriage with the French Bulldog, and the old musical on the TV. These should feel like domestic echoes of the Bagel before the Bagel is named.

3. **The laundry RV should split family business from Alphaverse machinery without losing the laundry core.** Scene 13 is racks of clothing, bags of laundry, and Waymond’s Tupperware of cookies. Scene 23 should not erase that history; layer wires, cables, monitor arrays, and Alpha Evelyn’s shrine with candles, portrait, and ornate urn into a vehicle that still feels converted from work, not designed as a clean sci-fi command center.

4. **IRS spaces should remain bland enough for absurd props to feel violent.** Scene 15 needs desks, files, receipts, cookies, protein shake, calculator, and Deirdre’s hand grip strengthener. Scene 16 adds Auditor of the Month trophies and a breakaway closet door. Scene 17 turns security batons, fish bowl pebbles, divorce papers, chapstick, and the fanny pack into combat materials. The set should support choreography while preserving the humiliation of being trapped in an audit.

5. **Executive spaces should reveal power as tacky, compromised, and usable.** The 9th floor executive office in scenes 67, 80, 85, and 124 needs the secret bookshelf door and a polish level above the cubicles, but scene 80’s panic room must undercut that polish with vents for gas, kinky toys, cocaine, porno mags, an Xbox, gas masks, riot shield, orange soda, spatulas, motorized feather toy, and Auditor trophies used as jump-pad objects.

6. **Cosmic spaces must keep one concrete, readable object at the center.** The empress hall in scene 91 can be grand and white, but the white veils, children’s book, black forehead circles on followers, wind, and Bagel reveal should keep it legible as Jobu’s ritual space. The rock field in scene 94 should be almost aggressively stripped down: two prominent rocks, dirt field, wind, and cliff edge. Its emptiness works because the previous spaces are overloaded.

7. **The final return should show that the ordinary world survived the cosmic collision.** In scene 118, the pull into the Bagel should carry karaoke microphone, linked family bodies, wind, and rapid universe resolution. In scene 124, the IRS office should still have protein shake and red sticky notes while Evelyn holds Waymond’s hand. The design point is not cleanup; it is choosing the same mundane room with a changed eye.

## Hero Objects

### Tax Receipts

**Scenes:** 2, 15.  
**Narrative job:** Receipts are Evelyn’s first visible antagonist. In scene 2, they cover the apartment table with years of tax documents; in scene 15, they return inside the IRS audit as the pressure becomes institutional.  
**Visual handling:** Use layered paper tones, curled edges, rubber bands, handwritten marks, calculator tape, and sorted-but-failing piles. They should look touched by Evelyn repeatedly, not artfully scattered.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Prepare hero readable receipts for close work, stunt-safe loose stacks for table disruption, and continuity bundles that can travel from apartment pressure to IRS audit pressure.

### Divorce Papers

**Scenes:** 2, 14, 17, 118.  
**Narrative job:** The private wound inside the public crisis. Hidden in scene 2, carried into the elevator in scene 14, discovered before the fanny pack fight in scene 17, and emotionally resolved around the family pull in scene 118.  
**Visual handling:** Keep them ordinary and bureaucratic, close enough to tax paperwork that Evelyn can miss them at first. The writing on the back for verse-jump instructions should feel urgent and practical, not mystical.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Need clean hero versions, folded pocket versions, marked instruction versions, and battle-worn continuity versions.

### Googly Eyes

**Scenes:** 6, 110.  
**Narrative job:** A small domestic joke becomes Evelyn’s chosen way of seeing. In scene 6 they sit on laundry plastic; in scene 110 Evelyn places one on her forehead and bullets transform into googly eyes.  
**Visual handling:** Treat them as cheap, tactile, adhesive craft objects. Their power comes from being unimportant until Evelyn gives them meaning. Avoid premium or custom-looking versions.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Stock many sizes for laundry dressing, baseball bat application, forehead hero placement, stunt-safe battlefield scatter, and VFX reference for bullet transformations.

### The Bagel

**Scenes:** 67, 91, 118.  
**Narrative job:** The object form of Jobu’s nihilism. It begins in Jobu’s hands in scene 67, becomes the veiled center of worship in scene 91, and threatens to take Joy in scene 118.  
**Visual handling:** It should be cosmic but not elegant. Its black-hole surface should still suggest food, circle, void, and joke at once. In scene 91, the white veils and wind should make its reveal feel ceremonial and suffocating.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Requires VFX integration, interactive light reference, wind-safe veil rigging, eyeline markers, and practical shadow/light behavior on faces and nearby fabric.

### Fanny Pack

**Scenes:** 14, 17.  
**Narrative job:** Waymond’s softness becomes tactical. Introduced with the elevator switch in scene 14, then weaponized in scene 17 as Alpha Waymond uses it like nunchucks.  
**Visual handling:** Keep it dad-like and slightly embarrassing before it becomes dangerous. The surprise depends on it looking like something from Waymond’s ordinary body, not a designed weapon.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Need a soft everyday version, weighted reinforced stunt versions, breakaway or repairable variants, and continuity versions for before and after the security guard fight.

### Bluetooth Earpieces

**Scenes:** 14, 16, 17, 25, 80, 110.  
**Narrative job:** The physical interface between mundane body and multiverse command system. They mark verse-jump mechanics across the elevator, closet, cubicles, 8th floor, panic room, and final atrium battle.  
**Visual handling:** Keep the devices small and consumer-tech adjacent, with practical LEDs in red, green, and yellow. The lights should read as instructions under pressure, not decorative sci-fi.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Build practical LED units, stunt-safe units, dead/non-lighting backups, and close-up hero pieces with consistent color logic.

### Baseball Bat

**Scenes:** 6, 110.  
**Narrative job:** Evelyn’s ordinary threat tool becomes part of the empathy system. In scene 6 she uses the handle to thump the dented air vent; in scene 110 it gains a googly eye as her violence turns toward repair.  
**Visual handling:** It should feel like a kept-behind-the-counter laundromat object, scuffed and practical. The googly eye addition should be visibly handmade and slightly funny.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Need rubber stunt versions, denting-safe contact versions, a hero scuffed version, and a googly-eye continuity version.

### Auditor Trophy

**Scenes:** 16, 80.  
**Narrative job:** Bureaucratic pride becomes an absurd body object and then a jump pad. It appears among Auditor of the Month trophies in scene 16 and returns as Auditor of the Year butt plug trophies in scene 80.  
**Visual handling:** It should live believably in an IRS office while carrying the inappropriate silhouette specified by the script. The joke should be in the shape and institutional engraving, not extra dressing.  
**Multiples/practical notes:** Need desk dressing trophies, hero close-up trophies, stunt-safe jump-pad versions, and matching set multiples for continuity.

## Wardrobe Logic

Wardrobe should track how each character is trapped by a life system before the multiverse exaggerates it. Evelyn begins in scene 2 as a 55-year-old Chinese woman in an oversized T-shirt, gray-streaked hair, and thin outdated designer glasses, surrounded by receipts and appliances; her clothes should feel like someone who has been interrupted by work for years. Waymond’s transition lens glasses and ill-fitting polo in scene 2 should make the fanny pack in scenes 14 and 17 feel fully of his body before Alpha Waymond weaponizes it. Joy’s worn-down hoodie and blank expression in scene 3 should contrast with Jobu’s constant costume instability: small boots, elaborate mask, and bloody bib in scene 18; Vegas-style Elvis jumpsuit with pig on a leash in scene 56; alien royalty in the white-veiled Bagel hall in scene 91. Deirdre’s cheap suit and hand grip strengthener in scene 15 should remain legible under her later physical threat, while Deirdre (Bagel) gets the sharpie circle on her forehead in scenes 18, 25, 56, 91, and 107 as a belief mark rather than a fashion mark. Gong Gong’s white undershirt, slippers, cane, and wheelchair in scenes 2 and 6 should stay grounded so Alpha Gong Gong’s office-supply robot exoskeleton in scene 110 feels like militarized family authority built from the same institutional junk world.

## Motif Continuity

The strongest continuity system is circles under pressure. Scene 1 begins with a round mirror holding karaoke happiness; scene 3 turns that circle into the washing machine Joy stares into; scene 6 plants googly eyes on laundry plastic; scene 67 puts a black swirling Bagel in Jobu’s hands; scene 91 reveals the Bagel behind white veils; scene 110 turns Evelyn’s forehead into a chosen third eye and bullets into googly eyes. Paper should form the second system: tax documents and receipts trap Evelyn in scene 2, receipts return during the audit in scene 15, paper becomes a verse-jump hazard with papercuts in scene 25, and divorce papers move through scenes 2, 14, 17, and 118 as the marriage’s hidden document. Cheap institutional surfaces should repeat across the IRS: cubicle walls in scenes 15 through 18, breakaway exit sign and filing cabinets in scene 25, trophies in scenes 16 and 80, banisters and break room in scenes 86, 101, and 110. White should be reserved carefully: Gong Gong’s undershirt in scene 6, the white veils in scene 91, and the stripped rock field in scene 94 should feel like different kinds of exposure, not a blanket purity palette.

## What To Avoid

Do not make the multiverse sleek. The script’s power comes from the compact umbrella covering an elevator camera in scene 14, chapstick being eaten in scene 17, office trophies becoming jump pads in scene 80, and a baseball bat gaining a googly eye in scene 110. Do not clean up the apartment or laundromat into tasteful poverty; scene 2 requires years of tax documents, receipts, workout equipment, rice cooker, calculator, and hidden divorce papers, while scene 6 needs quarters, laundry plastic, a dented air vent, a bat, a cane, and a baby carriage with a French Bulldog. Do not turn Jobu into a single polished villain silhouette; her threat is the overload from scene 18’s mask and bloody bib to scene 56’s Elvis jumpsuit and pig to scene 91’s alien royalty. Do not let the IRS become an action-movie arena; the fights need cubicles, files, trophies, sticky notes, protein shakes, and breakaway office architecture so Waymond’s kindness speech in scene 101 lands inside a place built for judgment. Do not overdesign the rock field in scene 94; two rocks, dirt, wind, and a cliff edge are the point.
