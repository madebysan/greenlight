# Poster Concepts: Everything Everywhere All At Once

## About This Document
These are concept directions for movie poster design. Each concept describes a visual approach, composition, and mood. Use these as briefs for a designer, or as prompts for AI image generation. Mix and match elements across concepts.

---

## Category: Character-Driven

**Concept 1: Evelyn's Exhaustion**
- **Style:** Close-up portrait with dimensional depth-of-field
- **Composition:** Evelyn's face fills 60% of the poster, shot slightly off-center. Her tired eyes are sharp and in focus, while fractured alternate universe versions of her face blur and multiply in the background—some smiling, some screaming, some in different costumes. A strand of gray hair falls across her forehead. The apartment clutter (rice cooker, self-help books, workout equipment) barely visible but textured behind her.
- **Color Palette:** #E8D5B7 (warm, exhausted skin tone), #2C3E50 (deep blue-gray background), #D4A574 (muted gold accents), #8B4513 (hint of brown from hair)
- **Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in bold sans-serif, 40% of poster width, positioned above her head with subtle blur effect on alternate letters. Tagline below chin in smaller italic serif.
- **Tagline:** "One life. Infinite choices. All the regret."
- **Mood:** Haunted, resigned, introspective
- **Target Appeal:** Adult drama audiences, character-study fans, Oscar-bait demographics
- **AI Prompt:** "Close-up portrait of a 55-year-old Chinese woman with tired eyes and gray streaks in her hair, wearing oversized clothing. Her face is sharp in focus while ghostly transparent versions of her face with different expressions multiply and blur behind her. Warm, muted color grading with a sense of existential exhaustion. High contrast lighting that emphasizes her wrinkles and weariness."

**Concept 2: Joy's Duality**
- **Style:** Split-face portrait with stark geometric division
- **Composition:** Joy's face divided vertically down the center. Left side shows her in a worn hoodie, blank stoic expression, real and grounded. Right side shows her as Jobu Tupaki—same face but with wildly exaggerated, shifting features, wearing an extravagant costume fragment, expression maniacal or ecstatic. The division line is sharp and electric, crackling with energy. Eyes lock forward in both halves.
- **Color Palette:** #1A1A1A (deep black), #FF6B9D (electric pink/magenta), #2A9D8F (teal-green accent), #FFFACD (pale yellow highlight)
- **Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" split across the division line—left side in clean sans-serif, right side in chaotic, glitchy typography. Title is 45% of poster width.
- **Tagline:** "Infinite worlds. One choice."
- **Mood:** Unhinged, duality, electric danger
- **Target Appeal:** Gen Z, sci-fi action fans, psychological thriller audiences
- **AI Prompt:** "Split-screen portrait of a woman's face divided vertically in half. Left side: stoic, blank expression, wearing worn hoodie, muted colors, realistic. Right side: same face but with wild exaggerated features, wearing colorful extravagant costume fragments, expression manic and extreme, neon colors. The division line crackles with electric energy and distortion."

**Concept 3: Waymond's Kindness**
- **Style:** Silhouette with warm interior light
- **Composition:** Waymond's full figure silhouetted against a warm, glowing background. He's positioned center-frame, wearing his ill-fitting polo shirt and transition glasses, one hand extended outward in an open, peaceful gesture. Inside his silhouette, soft golden light emanates—visible through his translucent edges—like light from within. The background is soft and diffused, almost dreamlike. His posture is gentle and vulnerable, not heroic.
- **Color Palette:** #FDB750 (warm golden interior glow), #1C1C1C (deep black silhouette), #F5E6D3 (cream background), #8B7355 (warm brown accent)
- **Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in elegant serif font, positioned above him, glowing slightly with the same golden light. Small subtitle below: "In infinite universes, kindness is still the answer."
- **Tagline:** "Be kind anyway."
- **Mood:** Hopeful, gentle, luminous
- **Target Appeal:** Families, older demographics, emotional drama audiences, awards consideration
- **AI Prompt:** "Silhouette of a man in an ill-fitting polo shirt and glasses, arms open in a peaceful gesture. His silhouette glows from within with warm golden light, like he's filled with hope and kindness. The background is soft, dreamy, and glowing. The overall mood is gentle, vulnerable, and luminous rather than heroic."

## Category: Symbolic/Metaphorical

**Concept 4: The Bagel**
- **Style:** Surreal, floating central object with dimensional abstraction
- **Composition:** A massive bagel dominates the center of the poster, but it's grotesque and ominous—dark, swirling, chaotic, almost like a black hole with texture and depth. The bagel is semi-transparent, revealing fractal patterns and distorted universe scenes within it. Small figures (barely perceptible) are being pulled toward it from multiple angles. The edges of the bagel blur and distort the reality around it. Background fades from realistic (bottom) to completely abstract and colorful (top), suggesting multiple universes colliding.
- **Color Palette:** #000000 (void black), #FF1744 (blood red accent), #FFD700 (gold distortion), #00BCD4 (electric cyan trails), #F5F5F5 (white void)
- **Typography:** Title in white sans-serif positioned above the bagel, letters slightly warped as if affected by gravitational pull. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" with letter-spacing increased for tension.
- **Tagline:** "The universe folds in on itself."
- **Mood:** Cosmic horror, chaos, existential threat
- **Target Appeal:** Sci-fi enthusiasts, cosmic horror fans, experimental cinema audiences
- **AI Prompt:** "A massive, grotesque bagel floating in the center of the frame, but it's dark, twisted, and ominous like a black hole. The bagel is semi-transparent, revealing fractal patterns and scenes of multiple universes swirling within it. Small figures are being pulled toward it. The background transitions from realistic at the bottom to completely abstract and colorful chaos at the top, suggesting reality breaking down."

**Concept 5: The Multiverse as Fabric**
- **Style:** Abstract textile/weaving metaphor with depth
- **Composition:** The poster surface appears to be made of interwoven threads or fabric, each thread representing a different universe. Threads are different colors, patterns, and textures—some showing laundromat scenes, some showing kitchen chaos, some showing absurd universes (hot dogs, rocks, etc.). The threads visibly weave, overlap, and tangle in the center, with some pulling apart and others snapping. The composition creates a 3D effect, as if you could reach in and grab the threads. Silhouettes of characters emerge from the weave pattern.
- **Color Palette:** #C41E3A (deep red thread), #1B4D3E (forest green thread), #DAA520 (golden thread), #4A4E69 (dark purple thread), #CCCCCC (white space between)
- **Typography:** Title woven INTO the fabric pattern itself, some letters visible, some hidden behind threads. The effect is subtle and integrated, almost like the title is part of the multiverse itself. 35% of poster width.
- **Tagline:** "Every choice unravels everything."
- **Mood:** Intricate, interconnected, fragile
- **Target Appeal:** Design-conscious audiences, art film enthusiasts, indie cinema lovers
- **AI Prompt:** "An intricate, close-up texture of woven threads or fabric in multiple colors (red, green, gold, purple, blue). Each thread represents a different universe and contains tiny scenes—a laundromat, a kitchen, absurd imagery. The threads visibly tangle, overlap, fray, and snap in places, creating a 3D woven effect. Silhouettes of human figures emerge from the weave pattern. The overall effect is like looking at a microscopic view of reality being woven and unraveled."

**Concept 6: Hands Across Universes**
- **Style:** Symbolic hand imagery with multiverse overlay
- **Composition:** Multiple hands emerge from different corners and edges of the poster, all reaching toward a central point—but the hands are from different universes (one hand is normal, one is a hot dog, one is made of light, one is painted/tattooed, etc.). The hands almost touch at center but don't quite meet, creating tension. Each hand casts a different colored shadow or glow. The background behind each hand shows a fragment of a different location (laundromat, IRS building, mystical temple, rock field). The negative space between the hands is the focal point.
- **Color Palette:** #FFC0CB (flesh pink), #FFD700 (hot dog yellow), #87CEEB (ethereal blue), #8B4513 (shadow brown), #000000 (void black center)
- **Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" positioned at the center point where the hands almost meet, small and elegant, almost whispered.
- **Tagline:** "In infinite worlds, we reach for the same thing."
- **Mood:** Connected, yearning, hopeful desperation
- **Target Appeal:** Emotional audiences, philosophy-minded viewers, family drama fans
- **AI Prompt:** "Multiple hands reaching from different corners of the frame toward a central point where they almost touch but don't meet. Each hand is from a different universe: one normal human hand, one made of bright yellow hot dog material, one made of glowing light, one tattooed and pierced, one skeletal. Each hand casts a different colored shadow or glow. Background fragments show different locations behind each hand. Tension and yearning in the composition."

## Category: Scene-Based

**Concept 7: The Laundromat Portal**
- **Style:** Iconic location shot with surreal energy
- **Composition:** The laundromat interior is recognizable and grounded in the foreground—laundry carts, the old arcade cabinet, worn seats—rendered in slightly desaturated, realistic colors. But from the center of the composition (perhaps the drum of a washing machine), reality explodes outward: fractured, multicolored, chaotic imagery bursts out like energy waves, showing glimpses of other universes (neon lights, cosmic scenes, absurd imagery). The explosion of color and strangeness radiates outward from the center. The contrast between the mundane foreground and the cosmic background is stark and dynamic.
- **Color Palette:** #8B8B7A (worn laundromat gray), #FFD700 (golden energy burst), #FF1744 (red chaos), #00BCD4 (cyan light), #2C3E50 (dark blue reality)
- **Typography:** Title positioned above the laundromat entrance or floating in the energy explosion, bold and impactful. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" spans 50% of poster width.
- **Tagline:** "Your ordinary life is extraordinary."
- **Mood:** Magical realism, hidden potential, ordinary-turned-cosmic
- **Target Appeal:** Broad audience appeal, families, indie film fans
- **AI Prompt:** "Interior of a worn, realistic laundromat with laundry machines, carts, old arcade cabinet, and worn seating. The foreground is grounded and desaturated. But from the center of the washing machine drum, explosive multicolored energy bursts outward—fractured reality showing glimpses of cosmic scenes, neon dimensions, and chaotic universes. The explosion of color radiates outward in waves, creating stark contrast with the mundane foreground. High-energy, magical realism aesthetic."

**Concept 8: The IRS Building Convergence**
- **Style:** Architectural sci-fi with character emergence
- **Composition:** The IRS building's brutalist architecture dominates the poster—cold, gray, geometrically strict. But bursting from multiple floors and windows are action sequences from different universe variants: from one window, someone kung-fu fights; from another, an opera singer emerges; from another, neon pink light explodes; from another, it's completely normal. Characters in different costumes and states of action tumble down the exterior, around the building, and through its structure. The building itself seems to be the nexus point where all universes collide. The composition is chaotic but compositionally balanced.
- **Color Palette:** #808080 (corporate gray), #00FF00 (neon action green), #FF69B4 (hot pink chaos), #FFD700 (golden kung-fu energy), #1C1C1C (shadow black)
- **Typography:** Title positioned as if it's part of the building's architecture, integrated into the grid structure. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in strong, rigid sans-serif, 45% width.
- **Tagline:** "Reality is breaking down."
- **Mood:** Chaos, convergence, action-packed density
- **Target Appeal:** Action film audiences, sci-fi adventure fans, younger demographics
- **AI Prompt:** "A stark, brutalist IRS office building in shades of gray, viewed from the exterior. Bursting from multiple windows and floors are chaotic action sequences: characters in different costumes fighting, kung-fu moves, opera singers, neon explosions. Characters and objects tumble down the exterior of the building in different action poses. The building appears to be the nexus point where multiple realities collide. Compositionally balanced chaos with high energy."

**Concept 9: The Quiet Moment**
- **Style:** Intimate, still-life drama
- **Composition:** Two rocks sit in an empty, windswept dirt field under a vast sky. The image is supremely calm and quiet—the epitome of minimalism and desolation. But overlaid as text or subtle visual elements are conversation bubbles or fragments of dialogue between the rocks (rendered as barely visible text). In the far background, small silhouettes of Evelyn and Waymond stand holding hands. The sky is enormous, taking up 60% of the poster. The overall effect is meditative, melancholic, and quietly profound. It's almost an anti-action poster.
- **Color Palette:** #D2B48C (tan earth), #87CEEB (soft sky blue), #8B7355 (rock brown), #FFFFFF (white space), #4A4A4A (subtle text gray)
- **Typography:** Title is extremely small and positioned at the bottom of the poster, in a quiet serif font. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in 20-point, subtle. Tagline is the focal point conversation between the rocks, rendered as barely-visible text overlay.
- **Tagline:** "Even in nothingness, there is meaning."
- **Mood:** Meditative, melancholic, profound, quiet
- **Target Appeal:** Adult drama audiences, arthouse cinema, contemplative viewers
- **AI Prompt:** "Two rocks sitting in an empty, windswept dirt field under an enormous, clear sky. The landscape is utterly quiet, desolate, and minimal. In the far background, two small silhouettes hold hands. The sky dominates the composition, taking up 60% of the frame. Soft, muted colors. A sense of profound stillness and meditation. Small text fragments float between the rocks like silent conversation."

## Category: Minimalist/Typographic

**Concept 10: The Infinite Regression**
- **Style:** Pure typography with recursive nesting
- **Composition:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" is the primary title, but each word is nested inside itself repeatedly, creating a fractal-like effect. The title itself becomes smaller and smaller, repeating infinitely toward a vanishing point at the center. The recursion creates a sense of infinite regression and looping. Behind the typography, a solid color field or gradient that subtly shifts from one universe's color to another. The effect is hypnotic and a bit dizzying. No imagery, only text and negative space.
- **Color Palette:** #2C3E50 (deep blue background), #FFFFFF (white text), #FFD700 (accent color at center point), gradient shift to #FF1744
- **Typography:** Custom, recursive typographic treatment. Title spans 70% of poster width. Each nested repetition is 85% the size of the previous. Tagline in small caps at bottom.
- **Tagline:** "Where it all collapses into itself."
- **Mood:** Intellectual, vertigo-inducing, abstract
- **Target Appeal:** Design-forward audiences, experimental cinema fans, festival circuits
- **AI Prompt:** "Pure typography design: The words 'EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE' repeated and nested infinitely, each repetition smaller than the last, creating a fractal-like recursive effect toward a vanishing point at the center. The typography is white against a deep blue gradient background that shifts subtly toward red at the center. Hypnotic, vertiginous effect. No imagery, only text and negative space."

**Concept 11: The Question Mark Universe**
- **Style:** Minimalist symbol with typographic integration
- **Composition:** A single enormous question mark dominates the poster, rendered in a bold sans-serif font. But the question mark is constructed from or fragmented with imagery from the film—the dot contains a tiny laundromat scene, the curve contains swirling multiverse colors, the stroke shows character silhouettes. The background is a soft, blurred wash of multiple colors (representing different universes bleeding together). The title "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" wraps around or integrates with the question mark shape. The composition is 70% negative space.
- **Color Palette:** #1A1A1A (dark question mark), #FFD700 (golden core), background gradient of #FF6B9D, #2A9D8F, #FDB750, #87CEEB (soft multicolor blend)
- **Typography:** Title integrates with the question mark itself, or appears below in elegant serif. 40% of poster width. Tagline as small caps to the side.
- **Tagline:** "If everything is possible, what matters?"
- **Mood:** Philosophical, inquiring, minimalist elegance
- **Target Appeal:** Thoughtful audiences, awards-consideration viewers, sophisticated demographics
- **AI Prompt:** "A single enormous question mark in bold sans-serif font, dominating the poster. The question mark is fragmented with subtle imagery: the dot contains a tiny scene, the curve shows multicolored swirls, the stroke contains silhouettes. The background is a soft, blurred wash where multiple universe colors blend together—gold, pink, teal, cream, blue. 70% negative space. Philosophical and minimalist."

**Concept 12: One Word Per Universe**
- **Style:** Distributed typographic composition
- **Composition:** The title words are separated and scattered across the poster, each word in a different corner or edge, each in a different universe's visual style and color palette. "EVERYTHING" in one corner rendered in laundromat fluorescent lights, "EVERYWHERE" in cosmic neon, "ALL" in elegant script over a rock field, "AT" in hot-dog yellow with strange texture, "ONCE" in glitchy digital font. The negative space between the words is large and significant—it's almost as important as the type. Subtle connecting lines or visual threads link the words, suggesting they belong to the same story.
- **Color Palette:** Varies per word, but unified with thin connecting threads in #808080
- **Typography:** Each word in a completely different typeface and style. Words are 60-100 points each, spread across the poster. Tagline centered in negative space.
- **Tagline:** "Connected across infinity."
- **Mood:** Fragmented, dispersed, unified in chaos
- **Target Appeal:** Design enthusiasts, experimental audiences, international markets (works across languages visually)
- **AI Prompt:** "Typography composition where the words 'EVERYTHING,' 'EVERYWHERE,' 'ALL,' 'AT,' and 'ONCE' are scattered in different corners and edges of the poster. Each word appears in a completely different typeface, color, and visual style representing a different universe—one neon and cosmic, one in laundromat fluorescent, one elegant and earthen, one in strange textures, one glitchy. Thin connecting threads link the words. Large negative space is the focal point. Unified in chaos."

## Category: Mood/Atmospheric

**Concept 13: The Shatter Effect**
- **Style:** Abstract destruction with controlled chaos
- **Composition:** The poster appears to be shattering or fragmenting from the center outward, like a pane of glass breaking. The cracks radiate from a central point (perhaps Evelyn's eye or the bagel) in geometric patterns. Each fractured section contains a different universe or image—realistic scenes, absurd imagery, cosmic scenes, mundane family moments—all slightly misaligned and at different angles. The cracks themselves glow with neon energy. The fragments overlap and overlap again, creating depth. The overall effect is simultaneously destructive and beautiful, chaotic yet controlled.
- **Color Palette:** #1C1C1C (dark background), #FFD700 (golden cracks), #FF1744 (red fragments), #00BCD4 (cyan light bleeding through cracks), #FFFFFF (white highlights)
- **Typography:** Title fractured across the shatter lines, some letters larger, some cracked, some glowing. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" spans 55% of poster. Tagline at bottom in one of the larger fragments.
- **Tagline:** "Reality breaks in infinite ways."
- **Mood:** Chaotic, catastrophic, beautiful destruction
- **Target Appeal:** Action audiences, younger demographics, visually striking appeal
- **AI Prompt:** "Abstract composition of a poster shattering like broken glass from the center outward. Each fractured section contains different imagery: realistic scenes, cosmic images, absurd scenes, family moments—all at slight misalignments and angles. Geometric cracks radiate outward, glowing with neon golden and cyan light. Fragments overlap, creating depth. Beautiful, controlled chaos. Dark background with luminous cracks."

**Concept 14: Multiverse Color Bleed**
- **Style:** Chromatic aberration and color separation as mood
- **Composition:** A central focal point (perhaps Evelyn's silhouette or a character) is rendered in full color and sharp focus. Radiating outward from the center, the image separates into its color channels—red, green, blue—each channel shifted slightly in different directions, creating a chromatic aberration effect that gets more pronounced toward the edges. The effect suggests reality is being split into its component parts. The background fades into a soft gradient. The composition has a dreamlike, unstable quality.
- **Color Palette:** Pure RGB separation with #FF0000 (red shift), #00FF00 (green shift), #0000FF (blue shift), #000000 (void background), #FFFFFF (blend highlights)
- **Typography:** Title positioned at the center focal point, rendered in white with subtle RGB shift effect itself. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in 50-point sans-serif.
- **Tagline:** "Reality is separated into its parts."
- **Mood:** Unstable, dreamlike, psychedelic, fragmented
- **Target Appeal:** Sci-fi audiences, visual effects enthusiasts, younger demographics, streaming platforms
- **AI Prompt:** "A central silhouette of a person in sharp focus and vibrant color. Radiating outward, the image separates into red, green, and blue color channels, each slightly shifted in different directions, creating a chromatic aberration effect that intensifies toward the edges. The effect suggests reality splitting into its component parts. Dreamlike, psychedelic, and unstable feeling. Dark void background fading to subtle gradient."

**Concept 15: Dimensional Depth Blur**
- **Style:** Bokeh and depth-of-field as visual metaphor
- **Composition:** A character (likely Evelyn or the family) is sharp and in focus in the foreground. Behind them, bokeh balls of light (representing other universes) blur and fade into the distance—hundreds of soft, out-of-focus circles of light in different colors. Each bokeh circle contains a hint of a different universe. The depth effect is extreme, suggesting infinite worlds stacked behind the visible one. The foreground character seems isolated and small against the infinite blurred worlds. The composition has melancholic depth.
- **Color Palette:** #E8D5B7 (warm character foreground), background bokeh in #FFD700, #FF6B9D, #2A9D8F, #00BCD4, #8B4513 (varied soft circles), #2C3E50 (deep void edges)
- **Typography:** Title positioned at bottom of frame in elegant serif, slightly out of focus to match the bokeh effect. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in 45-point. Tagline small and subtle above.
- **Tagline:** "Alone in infinite worlds."
- **Mood:** Melancholic, isolated, infinite, awe-struck
- **Target Appeal:** Emotional drama audiences, adult demographics, arthouse cinema
- **AI Prompt:** "A character in sharp focus in the foreground—warm, realistic, isolated-looking. Behind them, extending infinitely into soft focus, are hundreds of bokeh balls of light in different colors (gold, pink, teal, cyan, brown, purple). Each bokeh circle contains a hint of a different universe or scene. Extreme depth of field. The character appears small and isolated against the infinite blurred worlds. Melancholic, awe-struck mood."

## Category: Collage/Ensemble

**Concept 16: The Multiverse Stack**
- **Style:** Layered card-stack or composite of multiple universes
- **Composition:** The poster is designed as if you're looking at a transparent stack of cards or universe layers, each slightly offset and tilted. Each layer shows a different scene or universe: the laundromat, the IRS building, the rock field, the hot dog universe, the opera scene, etc. Some layers show characters, some show just environments. The layers are semi-transparent so you can see through multiple layers at once, creating depth and complexity. The composition is organized chaos—each layer visible but contributing to a unified whole. The title spans across multiple layers.
- **Color Palette:** Each layer has its own dominant color, but unified with transparency: #8B8B7A (laundromat gray), #FF6B9D (hot dog pink), #FFD700 (opera gold), #87CEEB (rock field blue), #2C3E50 (IRS building dark), blended through transparency
- **Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" positioned so the title spans across multiple transparent layers, with different portions in different opacities.
- **Tagline:** "Multiple realities. One truth."
- **Mood:** Complex, layered, overwhelming clarity
- **Target Appeal:** Design-conscious audiences, film enthusiasts, awards voters
- **AI Prompt:** "A composite design showing multiple transparent layers stacked and slightly offset, each layer showing a different scene or universe: laundromat, IRS building, rock field, cosmic scenes, absurd imagery. Each layer is semi-transparent so you can see through multiple at once, creating complex depth. The layers blend through transparency. Organized chaos with clear visual hierarchy. Professional, sophisticated composite."

## Poster Series Ideas

### Series Option 1: Character Universe Variants
Create five distinct poster variations, each focusing on a different character as the central figure:
- **Evelyn Poster:** Exhaustion and determination (Concept 1)
- **Joy/Jobu Poster:** Duality and chaos (Concept 2)
- **Waymond Poster:** Kindness and hope (Concept 3)
- **Gong Gong Poster:** Legacy and burden (similar style, showing him in wheelchair with Alpha Gong Gong looming behind)
- **Deirdre Poster:** Transformation and redemption (showing her in multiple costume states)

Each poster uses the same typography treatment and tagline framework but with character-specific imagery and color palettes. Works well for international markets, character merchandise, and social media character spotlights.

### Series Option 2: Teaser → Theatrical → Prestige Progression
- **Teaser Poster:** Concept 11 (The Question Mark) - Philosophical, mysterious, minimal. Releases 6 months pre-release.
- **Theatrical Poster:** Concept 7 (The Laundromat Portal) or Concept 8 (IRS Building Convergence) - Larger-scale, action-oriented, accessible. Primary marketing poster for theater lobbies.
- **Prestige/Awards Poster:** Concept 9 (The Quiet Moment) or Concept 13 (The Shatter Effect) - Artistic, contemplative, elevated. For awards season, specialty venues, retrospectives.

This creates a narrative arc in marketing that deepens as the film progresses through its theatrical run.

### Series Option 3: Universe-Specific Regional Variants
Different poster treatments for different markets, each emphasizing a different universe:
- **Western Markets:** Concept 8 (IRS Building) or Concept 7 (Laundromat) - Action, spectacle
- **Asian Markets:** Concept 1 (Evelyn's Exhaustion) or Concept 3 (Waymond's Kindness) - Family drama, emotional resonance
- **Awards Markets:** Concept 9 (Quiet Moment) or Concept 12 (One Word Per Universe) - Artistic, sophisticated
- **Youth/Streaming:** Concept 2 (Joy's Duality) or Concept 13 (Shatter Effect) - Visually striking, digital-first

## Production Notes

### Poster Dimensions & Scaling
- **Theatrical:** 27"x40" (standard) - All concepts scale to this format
- **Digital/Streaming:** 16:9 aspect ratio (1920x1080 minimum)
- **Social Media:** Square crop (1:1) - Concepts 1, 2, 4, 11, 12 scale down best
- **Thumbnail Size:** Concepts 4, 11, 13, 14 maintain visual clarity at 200x300px

### Concept Scaling Recommendations

**Full-Size Theater Lobby (27x40) Champions:**
- Concept 7 (Laundromat Portal) - Dynamic color explosion reads clearly at distance
- Concept 8 (IRS Building) - Architectural composition holds at scale
- Concept 16 (Multiverse Stack) - Layered complexity is impressive at full size

**Streaming/Digital (16:9) Champions:**
- Concept 1 (Evelyn's Exhaustion) - Close-up portrait works perfectly on screens
- Concept 14 (Chromatic Aberration) - Psychedelic effect enhances on bright digital displays
- Concept 15 (Bokeh Depth) - Depth-of-field effect is more impactful on screens

**Social Media/Thumbnail (Square, 1:1) Champions:**
- Concept 2 (Joy's Duality) - Symmetrical composition works as square
- Concept 4 (The Bagel) - Central circular object is naturally square-friendly
- Concept 11 (Question Mark) - Single symbol scales to any dimension
- Concept 12 (One Word Per Universe) - Can be adapted to square composition

### Multi-Market Adaptability
- **Concept 11** (Question Mark) and **Concept 12** (One Word) work globally with text-only or minimal imagery—easiest for international localization
- **Concepts 1, 2, 3** (Character-driven) are excellent for IMAX, specialty theatrical, and awards consideration—high perceived production value
- **Concepts 7, 8** (Scene-based) perform best on streaming platforms and digital marketing where motion variants and animated versions can extend the campaign
- **Concept 9** (Quiet Moment) is polarizing but awards-friendly—best for prestige venues and festival circuits

### Key Art Visibility Checklist
All concepts maintain legible title treatment, clear focal point, and distinct color separation across formats:
- ✓ Title readable at 8-point minimum (streaming)
- ✓ Focal point identifiable at 10% scale (thumbnails)
- ✓ Color separation ensures no color-blind visibility issues
- ✓ No critical information in outer 10% of frame (cropping safety)
