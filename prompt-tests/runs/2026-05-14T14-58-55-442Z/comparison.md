The "Before" version understands that the *tone* is the product. The "After" version confuses the *execution* of the tone with the tone itself.

The "After" version is a masterclass in production design thinking for a film like *Everything Everywhere All At Once*. It avoids all flagged failures:

The "After" version makes you better at your job.
