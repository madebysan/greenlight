# Title & Palette: Graphic Design Pass

## Title Read

The title must carry a **mechanical, overlapping exhaustion**. Set "EVERYTHING" and "ALL AT ONCE" as one compressed block, then **let "EVERYWHERE" physically sprawl** between them—stretched, tracking loose, as if the word itself cannot fit in one line. This is Evelyn at the dining room table in scene 2, drowning in tax receipts and rice cookers, the words of her life bleeding into each other. The title should feel **typeset by a machine that is breaking down**.

The weight should be **even and heavy** (bold sans-serif, near-black), but the spacing should be **uneven, jostled**—like the clutter in the apartment dining room ("a still life of chaos," scene 1). There is no hierarchy between the three words because for Evelyn, every universe is equally present and equally urgent.

## Typography Direction

**Display Type:** A single heavy grotesque (think: a forced-width, unmodulated sans-serif with slight mechanical irregularities). The typeface should evoke **bureaucratic signage—an exit sign, a government form—** but pushed to the point of distortion. No serifs, no warmth. **Set everything in caps**, but let the tracking break on "EVERYWHERE" (loose, almost dissociative).

**Secondary Type:** A clean, neutral sans-serif (e.g., Helvetica Neue Light or Akzidenz-Grotesk) for body text—nothing decorative. This is the type of the IRS 10th floor cubicle (scene 15-18): invisible, functional, the background hum.

**Spacing & Scale:** 
- Title: 60% of the frame width, breaking across three lines with "EVERYWHERE" pushed to the right, orphaned.
- Body: generous leading, 42-48pt for headers, 24pt for copy. **Avoid centering.** Left-align everything. The deck should feel like a document being processed, not a poster.

**Restraint:** No drop shadows, no gradients, no 3D extrusion. Flat color blocks. The only texture should be the **visual noise of overlapping opacity**—like the security feed monitors in the apartment (scene 1) ghosting over each other.

## Deck Identity Rules

- **Title page:** Full title in the display grotesque over a **Receipt Beige** field. No subtitle. No tagline. The only other element is a single **googly eye** placed at the bottom-right corner—small, almost forgettable, but present.
- **Section headers:** Display grotesque, white text on **Bagel Void Black** bars. Each header is a single word: "EVERYTHING" / "EVERYWHERE" / "ALL AT ONCE" — representing the three acts (chaos, expansion, resolution).
- **Body slides:** **Fluorescent Office Blue** background for structural information (budget, schedule, cast). **Stairwell Gray** for mood/character breakdowns. Let the backgrounds shift without warning—this is the deck's own multiversal jump.
- **Props/costume callouts:** Use **Hot Dog Pink** as a highlight accent for single items (e.g., a line drawing of the fanny pack in scene 14 or the Auditor trophy in scene 16). Never more than one accent per slide.
- **Mood/tonal slides:** Use **Karaoke Gold** for the "why this matters" conclusion. The gold should be saved for the ending slides—the "Barbie Girl" anchor—and never appear in the first half.

## Thumbnail Read

At postage-stamp size (a deck thumbnail, a poster on a phone screen), the only element that survives is **the heavy title block on beige with one white dot (the googly eye)**. The eye must be the single clear focal point—everything else is texture, not legibility.

The title should be **bold enough to be read at 6pt**: three words, no serifs, high contrast (black on beige). The googly eye needs to be **a perfect circle of pure white**—no iris, no pupil, just the blank plastic disc from scene 6. It is both a joke and a weapon.

Secondarily, the **Bagel Void Black** as a full-page background with a small, off-center white circle (the bagel) functions as a clear thumbnail for the "Empress Hall" (scene 91) sequence—the threat of nothingness as a single, clean shape.

---

## Avoid

1. **Decorative serifs or script fonts.** The film is about bureaucratic clutter becoming cosmic. A serif suggests literary prestige, which is the wrong signal. The type should feel like it was printed on a tax form.

2. **Gradients or lens flares.** The film's visual language is harsh, flat, and overlapping. A gradient implies depth that the IRS building (the primary battleground) does not have. Keep colors solid.

3. **Making the googly eye cute.** Do not anthropomorphize it. Do not make it wink, smile, or have an expression. It is a piece of plastic. Its power is in its emptiness, not its charm.

4. **Big taglines or pull quotes.** Do not add "A film about everything" or "The multiverse has never looked like this." The title is the only copy that matters. Let the googly eye and the beige field do the work.

5. **Over-articulating the three words.** Do not stack them, do not color-code them (EVERYTHING in blue, EVERYWHERE in red, etc). They are one sentence, one breath, one overwhelming experience. Split them only through tracking, not through hierarchy.
