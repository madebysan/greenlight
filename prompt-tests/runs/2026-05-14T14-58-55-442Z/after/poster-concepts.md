# Poster Concepts: Key Art Pass

## One-Sheet Thesis

The key art problem is visual contradiction: how to sell a film that is simultaneously a kung-fu action spectacle (scene 17), a mother-daughter drama (scene 118), an absurdist comedy about IRS audits (scene 15), and a nihilist meditation on meaninglessness (scene 94). Generic hero-on-white imagery fails because the film's protagonist is a 55-year-old exhausted laundromat owner in an oversized t-shirt (Evelyn, scene 1). The posters must make the mundane *feel* epic and the absurd *feel* devastating—a tax receipt must carry the same weight as a black hole bagel.

## Primary Directions

### Concept 1: THE THIRD EYE

**One-sheet logic:** The googly eye is the film's single most distilled visual—a $0.02 craft supply that becomes the weapon of radical kindness. This poster makes Evelyn's face the battlefield, with the googly eye on her forehead (scene 110) as the literal lens. It sells the absurd premise while centering the human face.

**Composition:** Extreme close-up on Evelyn's face, forehead dominant. On her forehead: one large, practical, slightly-misaligned googly eye. The eye reflects a miniature IRS cubicle interior (scene 15) in one pupil and a swirling bagel-vortex (scene 67) in the other. Her real eyes are half-lidded, exhausted. The googly eye is the only sharp, clear object in frame—everything else is slightly soft.

**Script evidence:** "Evelyn sticks a googly eye on her forehead like a third eye, turning all bullets fired at her into harmless googly eyes" (scene 110).

**Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" set small and centered below her chin, typewriter font. Tagline below: "The only way to win is to see." No actors' names.

**Thumbnail read:** A face with a giant googly eye. Instantly absurd, instantly legible.

**What not to include:** The bagel. Bullets. Any action element. No Waymond, Joy, or Jobu.

**AI image prompt:** "Photorealistic extreme close-up of an exhausted middle-aged Asian woman's face, forehead dominant. A single large googly eye is stuck to her forehead, slightly off-center. The googly eye's pupil reflects a chaotic cubicle office. Her natural eyes are tired, bags underneath. Soft focus. No makeup. Warm tungsten lighting from below. Shallow depth of field. Not comedic—somber and surreal. Film grain."

### Concept 2: THE BAGEL AND THE ROCK

**One-sheet logic:** The film's two most potent visual metaphors—the swirling nihilist bagel (scene 67) and the silent rocks (scene 94)—exist on opposite ends of the emotional spectrum. This poster places them together to sell the film's tonal range: cosmic annihilation vs. quiet stillness.

**Composition:** Split vertically. Left half: pure black with a single perfect circle of swirling CGI chaos—the Bagel. Right half: a single gray rock sitting in dust, harsh midday sun. The rock has a tiny sticker-eyeball applied to its surface. Between the two halves, at the seam, a thin sliver of warm gold light.

**Script evidence:** "Jobu opens her hands to reveal a swirling, churning black hole resembling a bagel" (scene 67). "Two silent rocks sit in an empty, windswept dirt field, conversing through text on screen" (scene 94).

**Typography:** Title set in the seam of gold light, letters breaking apart on the left side into dust particles, staying solid on the right. Small subtitle: "Nothing matters. That's okay."

**Thumbnail read:** Black circle vs. gray rock. Minimalist. Mysterious.

**What not to include:** Any human figure. The googly eye on the rock should be barely visible.

**AI image prompt:** "Split composition. Left side: absolute black void with a swirling bagel-shaped spiral of light and particles, deep space black hole aesthetic, high contrast, VFX. Right side: harsh daylight photograph of a single gray rock on dry cracked earth, dust motes in air, slightly desaturated. A tiny googly eye sticker is barely visible on the rock's surface. Thin vertical line of warm gold between them. No people. Cinematic 35mm anamorphic. Textures: cosmic plasma on left, granular dirt on right."

### Concept 3: THE CLUTTER PORTRAIT

**One-sheet logic:** Evelyn's apartment is described as a "still life of chaos" overpopulated with workout equipment, self-help books, security monitors, rice cooker, microwave (scene 1, 2, 4, 12). This poster makes the clutter the composition—Evelyn is drowning in objects before she drowns in universes.

**Composition:** Evelyn sits at the dining table, seen through a hazy mirror (echoing scene 1's "through a hazy mirror"). The table is buried in tax receipts (scene 2), a calculator, a half-eaten bowl of noodles (scene 4). Behind her: a rice cooker, a microwave, an old TV showing static, workout equipment, self-help book spines visible. Her reflection in the mirror is slightly different—she holds a baseball bat with a googly eye (scene 110) in the reflection, but not in reality.

**Script evidence:** "Tiny dining room overpopulated with workout equipment, self-help books, old TV, security feed, rice cooker, and microwave. A still life of chaos" (scene 1, 2, 4, 12).

**Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" written in red sticky notes (scene 124) taped across the bottom of the frame, slightly overlapping. Tagline: "She has a lot on her plate."

**Thumbnail read:** A messy apartment with a tired woman at a table covered in papers.

**What not to include:** Any multiverse elements. No kung fu. No Deirdre. No bagel. No googly eye visible except in the mirror reflection.

**AI image prompt:** "Wide shot of a cluttered small apartment dining room, seen through a slightly warped circular mirror. A tired Chinese woman in her 50s sits at a table buried in tax paperwork, receipts, a calculator. Background: rice cooker, microwave, old CRT TV, workout equipment, self-help books. Warm fluorescent overhead light. In the mirror reflection, her posture is different and she holds a baseball bat. Cinematic. Overwhelming visual noise. Color palette: beige, brown, faded yellow. Photorealistic but slightly surreal through mirror distortion."

### Concept 4: THE FANNY PACK KUNG FU

**One-sheet logic:** Waymond's fanny pack martial arts (scene 17) is the film's signature action beat—an absurd weapon made devastating. This poster sells the action movie promise while preserving the joke.

**Composition:** Low angle looking up at Waymond in mid-air, fanny pack extended like a nunchuck mid-swing. The background is a bland IRS cubicle (scene 15-17)—carpet tiles, filing cabinets, an "Auditor of the Month" trophy (scene 16) on a desk. Waymond's transition-lens glasses (character description) are darkened. His polo shirt is untucked. His expression is serene, not aggressive. A single googly eye is stuck to the fanny pack.

**Script evidence:** "Alpha Waymond... annihilates a squad of security guards using his fanny pack as nunchucks" (scene 17). "Fanny pack martial arts choreography" (Waymond special requirements).

**Typography:** Title in bold sans-serif, angled to match the swing trajectory. "EVERYTHING" top left, "EVERYWHERE" center, "ALL AT ONCE" bottom right. Tagline: "The multiverse needs a hero with good storage."

**Thumbnail read:** A man flying in the air with a fanny pack. Action pose but absurd object.

**What not to include:** Evelyn. Blood. The bagel. Any sci-fi VFX element. The fanny pack must look like a real suburban dad accessory, not a tactical belt.

**AI image prompt:** "Dynamic low-angle action shot of a middle-aged Chinese man in mid-leap, fanny pack extended as a weapon like nunchucks. Background: bland corporate IRS cubicle with beige carpet tiles, filing cabinets, a trophy on a desk. The man wears an ill-fitting polo shirt and transition-lens glasses that have darkened. One googly eye stuck to the fanny pack. Motion blur on fanny pack. Soft fluorescent overhead lighting. Not glamorous—slightly dorky but cool. Cinematic wide lens."

### Concept 5: THE FAMILY LINKED ARMS

**One-sheet logic:** The climax—Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong linking arms to pull Joy from the Bagel (scene 118)—is the film's emotional thesis. This poster sells the family drama without revealing the absurd mechanics.

**Composition:** A tight, low three-quarter shot of three pairs of arms linked at the elbows. Hands interlocked. The top of the frame is the black void of the Bagel's pull. The bottom of the frame is Evelyn's apartment dining room floor—the same floor from scene 2 covered in tax receipts. The arms are in different sleeves: Evelyn's oversized t-shirt (character description), Waymond's ill-fitting polo, Gong Gong's white undershirt. One single googly eye is stuck to Evelyn's wrist.

**Script evidence:** "As Jobu is sucked into the Bagel, Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong link arms to physically pull her out of the darkness" (scene 118).

**Typography:** Title set small, centered in the black void at top. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in white. Below the arms: "Hold on." No other text.

**Thumbnail read:** Three pairs of arms linked together. Simple human image. The googly eye is a small detail.

**What not to include:** Joy's face. The actual bagel. Any action elements. No googly eye except the one on the wrist.

**AI image prompt:** "Tight shot of three pairs of arms linked at the elbows, hands interlocked, pulling. Top half of frame is pure black void. Bottom half shows a cluttered apartment floor with scattered receipts and papers. One arm sleeve is an oversized faded t-shirt, one is an ill-fitting polo shirt, one is a white undershirt. A single googly eye is stuck to the t-shirt sleeve just above the wrist. Tension in the arms. Naturalistic lighting. Film grain. Emotional, not action-oriented."

### Concept 6: THE HOT DOG FINGERS MOMENT

**One-sheet logic:** The Hot Dog Universe (scene 62) is the film's most absurd visual—long floppy fingers that become a tool for empathy when Evelyn wipes Deirdre's tears with her toes (scene 107). This poster sells the grotesque humor and the emotional pivot in one image.

**Composition:** Close-up of two hands, one human and one with hot dog fingers, touching at the fingertips. The hot dog fingers are slightly pink, slightly shiny, with a ketchup-like smear on one. The background is a split: left side is the dark IRS cubicle (scene 62), right side is warm golden light suggesting empathy.

**Script evidence:** "Evelyn looks down in horror to discover her fingers are long, floppy hot dogs" (scene 62). "Evelyn... wiping Deirdre's tears with her toes in the Hot Dog Universe" (scene 107).

**Typography:** Title set vertically along the seam between the two backgrounds. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE." Tagline: "Sometimes you have to get a little weird."

**Thumbnail read:** Two hands touching, one with hot dog fingers. Grotesque and compelling.

**What not to include:** Faces. The IRS setting. Waymond. The googly eye. No gore—the hot dogs should look like food, not body horror.

**AI image prompt:** "Close-up of two hands touching at the fingertips. Left hand: normal human hand with short nails. Right hand: fingers are long, pink, floppy hot dog sausages, slightly shiny, one with a small ketchup smear. Background split: left side dark corporate interior, right side warm golden light. Soft focus. Not gory—the hot dog fingers should look like actual food. Surreal but emotionally tender. Cinematic macro photography."

### Concept 7: THE IRS BUILDING AS BATTLEFIELD

**One-sheet logic:** The IRS building is the primary battleground across multiple universes (scenes 15-18, 25, 56, 67, 80, 85, 86, 101, 110, 124). This poster sells the film as an action epic set in the most boring place possible.

**Composition:** Wide vertical shot of the IRS building's exterior, but seen through a fisheye lens that makes it loom. The building is intact on the bottom floors, but the top floors are crumbling into the Bagel's vortex swirling in the sky above. Tiny silhouettes of Evelyn (holding baseball bat from scene 110), Waymond (with fanny pack from scene 17), and Jobu (in Elvis jumpsuit from scene 56) on the roof. The "Auditor of the Month" trophy (scene 16) is cropped in the foreground, huge.

**Script evidence:** "IRS building becomes the stage for kung fu, pro wrestling, and mech-suit battles" (Creative Director). "Auditor of the Month trophy... used in combat" (props master).

**Typography:** Title carved into the building's facade like a government plaque. "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" in serif font. Tagline below: "The audit is over."

**Thumbnail read:** A government building being destroyed by a black hole. Surreal.

**What not to include:** Evelyn's face visible. The rock universe. The laundromat. No googly eyes visible.

**AI image prompt:** "Wide fisheye shot of a generic government office building. Bottom floors normal with windows and doors. Top floors breaking apart into a swirling black hole vortex in the sky. Three tiny silhouettes on the roof: one holding a baseball bat, one with a visible fanny pack, one with wild costume. A large gold trophy in extreme foreground. Bright daylight on building, dark cosmic sky above. Surreal. Photorealistic architecture with VFX destruction. Not comic book style—cinematic."

### Concept 8: THE KARAOKE FAMILY

**One-sheet logic:** The film opens with the family awkwardly singing "Barbie Girl" (scene 1) and ends with that same song playing as they pull Joy from the Bagel (scene 118). This poster sells the family drama with the absurd pop-song context.

**Composition:** Family of four (Evelyn, Waymond, Joy, Gong Gong) standing at a karaoke machine, seen through a hazy disco-lit mirror (mirror from scene 1). Their expressions are strained but trying to be happy. Behind them in the mirror reflection: the IRS cubicle battleground (scene 17), two rocks on a field (scene 94), the Bagel (scene 67). The family's karaoke microphone has a googly eye stuck to it.

**Script evidence:** "Through a hazy mirror, a Chinese family of three awkwardly but sweetly sings karaoke together" (scene 1). "Evelyn, Waymond, and Gong Gong link arms to physically pull her out of the darkness" (scene 118). "The film opens with the family awkwardly singing 'Barbie Girl'" (Creative Director).

**Typography:** "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE" written in disco-ball sparkle text. Below: "A love song." No tagline.

**Thumbnail read:** A family singing karaoke with weird stuff in the mirror.

**What not to include:** The actual "Barbie Girl" title. Blood. Deirdre. The pig. No explicit violence.

**AI image prompt:** "Interior shot of a family of four standing at a karaoke machine in a small cluttered apartment. Seen through a hazy circular mirror with disco lights. A Chinese woman in her 50s, a man in a polo shirt, a young woman in a hoodie, an elderly man with a cane. Strained smiles. In the mirror reflection behind them: surreal images of an office cubicle fight, two rocks in a field, a cosmic bagel. A karaoke microphone has a googly eye sticker. Warm nostalgic lighting. Slightly grainy. Photorealistic but magic-realist."

## Teaser Directions

### Teaser 1: THE GOOGLY EYE ONLY

Black field. Center: a single googly eye sticker. White text below: "EVERYTHING." Then fade to black. "EVERYWHERE." Fade. "ALL AT ONCE." Fade. A beat. Then: "Look closer." The sticker remains. No faces, no tagline.

### Teaser 2: THE RECEIPT

Close-up of a single crumpled tax receipt (scene 2) on a cluttered table. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal the receipt is covered in tiny handwriting—instructions for multiversal verse jumping (scene 15). The receipt sits next to a single googly eye. Title text only: "Coming soon." Typography: typewriter font.

### Teaser 3: THE ROCK

Static shot of two rocks on an empty dirt field (scene 94). Wind blows dust. After 10 seconds, text appears between the rocks: "...Everything matters." Then a beat. "...Nothing matters." Fade to black. Title card: "EVERYTHING EVERYWHERE ALL AT ONCE." The googly eye sticker from the laundry RV (scene 6) appears on the title card as a watermark.

## Series Logic

**Phase 1 (Teaser):** Minimalist, mysterious. The googly eye. The receipt. The rocks. No humans, no action. Sell the curiosity.

**Phase 2 (Character Posters):** The Third Eye concept (Evelyn). The Fanny Pack (Waymond). The Bagel (Jobu). The Linked Arms (family). Four posters released weekly, each introducing a character through the film's visual language.

**Phase 3 (One-Sheets):** The Clutter Portrait as the primary theatrical poster. Wide release. The Bagel and Rock as alternative quad. The IRS Building as premium format (IMAX, Dolby) one-sheet.

**Phase 4 (Final Push):** The Karaoke Family poster for awards season. Emphasizes the tenderness. The Hot Dog Fingers as a cult/word-of-mouth poster for late-run.

## Best Bets

**Rank 1: The Third Eye.** This is the most conceptually clean image. It centers the film's protagonist, uses the googly eye (the most recognizable and cheapest prop), and sells the absurd premise without revealing plot. It works in thumbnail, in a subway ad, and as a social media avatar. It also protects the film's spoilers—you can't tell what universe she's in.

**Rank 2: The Clutter Portrait.** This sells "this is a movie about an overwhelmed woman" before "this is a movie about the multiverse." The detail of the baseball bat in the mirror reflection rewards repeat viewing. It's a great "I don't know anything about this movie" poster.

**Rank 3: The Bagel and the Rock.** This is the most art-house option and works best for festival campaigns or critics' screenings. It signals "this is not a standard action movie." It's the most tonally accurate to the film's third-act philosophical shift. Weakness: it doesn't sell action or comedy.
